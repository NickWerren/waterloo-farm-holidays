## Marazion Marsh

The RSPB's most southerly reserve, Marazion Marsh provides crucial reedbed habitat for wetland birds. The reserve offers excellent viewing opportunities year-round with easy access and well-maintained paths.

### Key Features

- Extensive reedbeds
- Open water areas
- Viewing hides
- Boardwalk access

### Target Species

- Reed Warbler (summer)
- Sedge Warbler (summer)
- Water Rail
- Little Egret
- Various duck species (winter)
- Migrant waders
- Kingfisher

### Visiting Information

The reserve has good accessibility with level paths and viewing hides. Best visited early morning or late afternoon. Free entry as an RSPB reserve.
