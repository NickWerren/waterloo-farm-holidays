Swimming in Cornwall

From sandy beaches to hidden rivers and dramatic waterfalls - discover incredible swimming opportunities near Waterloo Farm.

Safety Warning

Wild swimming and sea swimming carry inherent risks. Please read this safety information carefully before swimming.

Water Safety:

  • Never swim alone - always swim with others
  • Check weather conditions and tide times before entering the water
  • Be aware of currents, rip tides, and undertows, especially at beaches
  • Cold water shock is a serious risk even in summer - enter gradually
  • River levels can rise rapidly after rain - check conditions
  • Waterfalls and river pools may have submerged rocks and hazards
  • Wear appropriate gear including wetsuit in cold conditions

Beach Swimming:

  • Only swim at lifeguarded beaches where possible
  • Swim between the red and yellow flags at lifeguarded beaches
  • Never swim near cliffs or rocky outcrops
  • Be aware of tide times - some beaches become dangerous at high tide
  • Check for warning signs and flags before entering

Wild Swimming:

  • Research locations carefully - not all spots are safe for swimming
  • Water quality can vary - avoid swimming after heavy rain
  • Be cautious of hidden currents in rivers and estuaries
  • Waterborne diseases (Weil’s disease) can be present in fresh water
  • Never dive into water unless you know it’s deep and clear of hazards
  • Tell someone where you’re going and when you’ll return

If you’re unsure about conditions, don’t swim. Your safety is paramount.
