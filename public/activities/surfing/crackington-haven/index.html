Crackington Haven

Posted by Waterloo Farm Holidays on Wednesday, November 5, 2025

Crackington Haven

Distance: 25 minutes (12 miles) | Level: Intermediate to Advanced | Type: Cove Beach, Reef Break

Beautiful cove with powerful waves when the swell is up - for experienced surfers.

Beach Overview

Crackington Haven is a stunning cove surrounded by dramatic cliffs. When conditions are right, it offers excellent surf, but the powerful waves and reef breaks make it better suited to experienced surfers. Less crowded than nearby Bude beaches.

Surf Conditions

  • Best Tide: Mid to high tide
  • Swell Direction: SW to W
  • Wave Type: Beach and reef breaks
  • Bottom: Sand and rock
  • Needs: Bigger swell to work properly
  • Crowding: Less crowded, locals spot

Facilities

  • Parking: Pay & display car park
  • Lifeguards: Seasonal (summer)
  • Toilets: Yes
  • Café: Small café at car park
  • Surf Hire: Limited
  • Showers: None

Best For

  • Experienced Surfers: Powerful waves
  • Less Crowds: Quieter than Bude
  • Scenic Beauty: Stunning location
  • Big Swell Days: Works well when surf is up

Hazards & Safety

  • Rocky at low tide - check conditions
  • Powerful waves when big
  • Rip currents - be aware
  • Watch for rocks on takeoff
  • Reef sections - know your limits

Getting There

From Waterloo Farm: 25 minutes via B3314 towards Crackington Haven.

Nearby

  • Coombe Barton Inn (excellent food)
  • Coastal walk to Cambeak
  • Rock pools for non-surfers
  • Stunning cliff scenery

Best Time

  • Autumn/Winter: Best swells, experienced only
  • Summer: Smaller, safer waves
  • Spring: Good conditions, fewer crowds
  • Big Swells: When other beaches are too big

A beautiful surf spot for experienced surfers. The dramatic setting and quality waves make it worth the visit when conditions are right. Check the swell before making the trip.
