## Cardinham Woods

**Distance:** 1-4 miles (various trails) | **Duration:** 1-2 hours

A beautiful Forestry England woodland with waymarked trails for all abilities, from easy riverside strolls to longer forest hikes.

### Highlights

- Waymarked trails through mixed woodland
- Riverside paths along the Cardinham Water
- Excellent café at the woods entrance
- Mountain bike trails alongside walking routes
- Bluebells in spring, autumn colours in October
- Peaceful and sheltered even on windy days

### Route Details

**Starting Point:** Cardinham Woods car park, near Bodmin (PL30 4BB)
**Terrain:** Well-maintained forest paths, some muddy sections after rain
**Best Time:** Year-round (sheltered woodland)

### Trail Options

- **Lady Vale Bridge Walk (1 mile):** Easy, flat riverside stroll suitable for pushchairs and wheelchairs
- **Lidcutt Walk (2 miles):** Gentle woodland loop with valley views
- **Callywith Walk (3 miles):** Moderate circuit through the wider forest
- **Wilderness Walk (4 miles):** Longer loop taking in the deeper woodland and streams

### Facilities

- **Parking:** Pay & display car park (Forestry England)
- **Toilets:** Yes, at car park
- **Café:** Woods Café -- excellent homemade food and cakes
- **Dogs:** Welcome on leads

### What to Bring

- Sturdy footwear (paths can be muddy)
- Waterproof jacket
- Water and snacks for longer trails

### Getting There

From Waterloo Farm: approximately 25 minutes (14 miles). Head south on the A30 past Launceston, then take the A38 towards Bodmin. Follow signs to Cardinham Woods from the Cardinham turning.

**A lovely sheltered walk for any weather. The combination of beautiful woodland, riverside paths, and the excellent café makes this a favourite for families and dog walkers alike.**
