## Cornish Lanes Explorer

**Distance:** 15 miles | **Difficulty:** Easy | **Type:** Road

Perfect for families - quiet lanes through typical Cornish countryside.

### Highlights

- Traditional farms and countryside
- Wildlife spotting
- Peaceful narrow lanes
- Village pubs for refreshments
- Quintessential Cornwall scenery

### Route Details

**Starting Point:** North Petherwin
**Terrain:** Gentle rolling hills
**Best Time:** Year-round
**Estimated Time:** 1.5-2 hours
**Surface:** Quiet country lanes

### Description

This gentle route explores the classic Cornish landscape of narrow hedge-lined lanes, traditional farms, and small villages. Perfect for families or those wanting a relaxing ride without significant climbs. The route stays on quiet lanes with minimal traffic.

### Points of Interest

- **Traditional Cornish Hedges** - Ancient banks topped with hedges
- **Farmland Views** - Working farms and pastoral scenes
- **Wildlife** - Buzzards, rabbits, deer
- **Village Churches** - Historic parish churches
- **Stone Barns** - Traditional agricultural buildings

### Route Tips

- **Traffic:** Very quiet - ideal for families
- **Services:** Village pub in North Petherwin
- **Safety:** Lanes can be narrow - take care on blind bends
- **Pace:** Leisurely - stop and enjoy the scenery
- **Children:** Suitable for confident child cyclists

### What to Bring

- Water and light snacks
- Basic tools and spare tube
- Camera
- Picnic (optional - plenty of spots to stop)

### Family-Friendly Features

- Short distance manageable for children
- Minimal hills
- Very quiet roads
- Close to Waterloo Farm (easy bailout if needed)
- Village pub for refreshments

**Ideal for families, leisurely riders, or anyone wanting to experience peaceful Cornish lanes without the challenge of longer routes.**
