Oakside Fishery

A purpose-built carp fishing venue offering quality fishing for specimen carp in a peaceful, well-maintained environment. Oakside provides excellent facilities for both day ticket anglers and those seeking longer sessions in pursuit of larger fish.

Fishing Information

Species: Carp (commons and mirrors), tench Methods: Hair rigs, boilies, pellets, particles - modern carp fishing methods Season: Year-round Day Tickets & Sessions: Day tickets and 24-hour sessions available - booking advised Facilities: Car parking, toilets, well-maintained swims, some with night fishing facilities

The Fishery

Oakside features purpose-built lakes designed specifically for carp fishing. The venue is managed to high standards with regular maintenance and careful fish management ensuring healthy fish stocks and consistent fishing quality.

Fish Stocks

The lakes contain a good head of carp including both common and mirror varieties. Fish weights range from low doubles to specimens over 20lb. The fishery also holds a population of quality tench, which provide excellent sport during spring and summer months.

Fishing Approach

Location - The lakes feature various features including islands, margins, and deeper channels. Spend time observing fish movements before selecting your swim.

Tactics - Modern carp fishing methods are most effective. Hair-rigged boilies, method feeder approaches, and particle baits all produce fish. Pre-baiting can significantly improve catch rates.

Time of Day - Early morning and evening sessions are typically most productive, though fish can be caught throughout the day.

Facilities

Well-maintained fishing platforms provide comfortable fishing positions. Some swims are equipped for night fishing with level areas for bivvies. Vehicle access to swims is designed to make unloading tackle easy. The peaceful rural location provides an excellent atmosphere for longer sessions.

Etiquette & Rules

  • Barbless or micro-barbed hooks only
  • Unhooking mats and landing nets mandatory
  • All fish to be treated with respect and care
  • Keep noise to minimum, especially during night fishing
  • Take all litter home
  • Respect other anglers and maintain adequate distance between swims

Ideal For

  • Specimen carp anglers seeking quality fish
  • Those preferring a quieter, more peaceful venue
  • Anglers wanting to fish 24-hour sessions
  • Anglers who appreciate well-maintained, purpose-built facilities

Top Tips

  • Book in advance, especially for weekends and holidays
  • Bring a variety of baits - fish preferences can change
  • Location is crucial - watch for showing fish
  • Early season tench fishing can be exceptional
  • Respect the fishery rules - they ensure quality fishing for all
